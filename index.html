Xingru Chen

I study human-computer interaction, AR, and AI — driven by an open question: what would the next generation of interaction feel like if it were genuinely warmer, more attuned to people, more in tune with how we inhabit the world? I am a third-year undergraduate, expecting to graduate in June 2027, and am looking for PhD or Master’s programs starting Fall 2027.

Beijing, China

About

I am an undergraduate researcher in Computer Science (Digital Media Technology) at Beijing Forestry University, currently working at the Institute for AI Industry Research (AIR) and the Pervasive HCI (PI) Lab at Tsinghua University, and spending summer 2026 as a Mitacs Globalink Research Intern at the University of New Brunswick.

My research explores new paradigms for human-computer interaction at the intersection of AR, embodied AI, and multimodal sensing. I am drawn to the question of how computing can feel less like a tool and more like something that understands the human body, attention, and intent — warmer, and more naturally woven into how we move through the world.

My goal is to design interaction systems that are not merely efficient, but genuinely human — ones that sense, adapt to, and resonate with the people who use them.

Publications

CHI 2026 · Poster

3D-MAGIC: Expanding MAGIC Pointing to Stereoscopic Displays

Xingjian Tian, Lily Sheng, Xiyuxing Zhang, Xingru Chen, Fangfei Gou, Zhenzhou Zhang, Wanjun Lv, Liuxin Zhang, Yuntao Wang, Yuanchun Shi

We extend the MAGIC (Manual And Gaze Input Cascaded) pointing paradigm to stereoscopic 3D displays using Bayesian gaze-depth estimation, enabling fast and precise target acquisition. A user study (N = 10) showed a 20.7% throughput improvement over mouse-only interaction in immersive settings.
